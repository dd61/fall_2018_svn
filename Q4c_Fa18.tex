\question A tank holds 100 gallons of water, which drains from a leak at the bottom, causing the tank to drain in 40 minutes. The volume of water remaining in the tank after $t$ minutes is $\displaystyle V=f(t)=100\left (1-\frac{t}{40}\right )^2$.
\begin{parts}
	\part[2]  Find $\displaystyle f^{-1}(25)$. Show your work step by step.
	\fillwithdottedlines{2cm} 
	\part[1] What does $\displaystyle f^{-1}(25)$ represent? 
	\begin{choices}
		\choice $\displaystyle f^{-1}(25)$ represents the rate at which the tank is leaking when 25 gallons have drained from the tank.
		\choice $\displaystyle f^{-1}(25)$ represents the volume of water that has leaked out of the tank after 25 minutes.
		\choice $\displaystyle f^{-1}(25)$ represents the time that remains for the tank to be emptied when 25 gallons have drained from the tank.
		\choice $\displaystyle f^{-1}(25)$ represents the time that has elapsed when there are 25 gallons left in the tank. 
		\choice $\displaystyle f^{-1}(25)$ represents the water remaining in the tank 25 minutes after the tank started to leak.
	\end{choices}
\end{parts}
